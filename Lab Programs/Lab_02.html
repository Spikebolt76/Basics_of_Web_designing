           DNS, or Domain Name System, is like the phonebook of the internet. When you type a website address (like
        "google.com") into your browser, DNS translates that address into a number called an IP address (like
        142.250.190.78), which computers use to find and connect to each other on the internet.

           In short, DNS helps your computer understand where to find the website you want to visit by turning
        easy-to-remember names (like "amazon.com") into complex numbers that computers can understand.
